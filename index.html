Hi, I'm Mary! Thanks for visiting my portfolio website. Here is my resume.

I'm a 21-year-old journalist and social media strategist studying journalism and music at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.

In my reporting, I aim to bring readers on a journey through places, communities and ideas that they may not have encountered before. I dig beyond the "what" and investigate the "why" and the "how." And I strive to do it all with a writing style that’s engaging, compelling and accessible to all.

I stumbled into this profession during my sophomore year of college, when I applied to UNC’s student-run newspaper, The Daily Tar Heel, as a copy and social media staffer. (It was a good fit for me—though I’d never written a real news article, I was already a grammar stickler who spent too much time on Twitter.) As I copyedited and tweeted my heart out, I saw firsthand how journalism could serve communities and create change. I wanted in. I moved into feature writing, where I made a name for myself with my creative approach and earned the newsroom’s vote for “Best Baby-Staffer.”

I developed a voracious appetite for knowledge and experience in this industry. I secured a digital-content internship with local progressive newspaper INDY Week and a promotion to audience engagement editor at The Daily Tar Heel. Now, I’m dipping my toes into freelance reporting while I finish up my degree from the Hussman School of Journalism and Media, where I’ve recently earned a merit scholarship.

My next stop is a summer internship with the media company Red Ventures, where I’ll write and edit tech journalism for CNET. I’m set to graduate from UNC in December 2021. And after that? Who knows!

When I'm off assignment, I enjoy studying classical voice and honing my web development skills. (I coded this website myself using HTML/CSS and Bootstrap—I’m a beginner, but I poured my heart into it!)
